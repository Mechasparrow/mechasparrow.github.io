ACT prep: A post-mortem

Hey, everybody. This is a follow-up to a post I wrote 3 weeks back. You can check the previous one at https://medium.com/@mechasparrow/started-act-prep-8deb917472eb.

Well, I have finally completed the program. As a result, I would like to cover what the prep did and did not cover.

What it did cover

Throughout the session, usage of example packets and tactics were prominent. The usefulness of the test taking strategies is still questionable.

I have a personal bias in this since I did not see an improvement by using the tactics that they taught us.

Example packets and problems helped us get a better feel for the type of questions and problems that we could encounter on the test. This helped the most in the Science and English section.

What they did NOT cover

They did not provide a useful guide relating to pacing and timing of the test. They said you have x questions and y time so go complete everything in about y/x pacing.

They also did not cover any online resources to use for studying for the ACT during or after the prep session. This would help students actually use the information rather than absorb it.

Well, that was my short post-mortem on the summer ACT-prep course I took. If you liked it, please be sure to recommend, follow, and share.
