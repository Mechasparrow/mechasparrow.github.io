The Solar Eclipse

Hey, everyone. I just returned from the Fujitsu Planetarium in Cupertino, California. There we watched a special show about the American Eclipse.

This solar eclipse will occur on August 21 this year. The show talked about some interesting points about the eclipse.

Here are some of them.

Eclipses usually come in pairs

When a solar eclipse occurs, there is usually a lunar eclipse before or after it. This has to do with the alignment of the Earth, Moon, and Sun alongside their orbits.

A solar eclipse helped prove Einstein’s general theory of relativity

Yes. One of Einstein’s predictions about the general theory of relativity can be proven with a solar eclipse. This is exactly what they did.

Two expeditions were made to measure the stars under this prediction.

Solar eclipses (especially totality) are rare to occur

Many factors play a role in a solar eclipse. Here are a couple.

  • Whether the Moon is the right distance.
  • If the Moon is at a lunar node where it intersects with the ecliptic. This is the path the Sun takes across the sky.
  • The Moon must also be a new moon at a lunar node.

With all the requirements met, a solar eclipse can then occur.
