Fun (virtual) company stuff and Bread co

Today was an excellent day at school. I managed to avoid the several tests that I discussed of in yesterday’s blog post. However I would have to say the interesting parts of the school day was Virtual Enterprise class. As I have discussed in previous blog posts. Virtual Enterprise as a class where students participate in a virtual firm. Along with being assigned the role of web designer, I was also given the responsibility of department head of tech.

Benefits of being head of tech

  • get to boss upperclassmen around
  • Be an important voice in department meetings
  • Interact with other departments and manage tasks

I heavily enjoy this. The great part was one of the first investors of St. Louis Bread co came to give a talk to the class. This led to getting some interesting insight about business.

what was learned from the talk

One of the key points that I took away from talk was that switching gears is not that hard if you have the correct base skills. This is especially true for someone who is taking a business major. That would be because the skills you learn in business school can be applied to almost any business model. Such example would be marketing, real estate, accounting, etc.

Another point that was brought up was that of knowing the company to join. If you are to join a company that isn’t really growing anymore or is stable and has a stable stream of customers, the amount of growth in your career will not be as much. However if you join a small growing business, you will encounter more growth opportunities.

My third and final point that I took away is that first risk you take in business you take is always the hardest. After that the risks don’t seem as bad in the long run.

Well that’s it for blog post. I had this awesome idea during this blog post. I will make sure to discuss tomorrow.
