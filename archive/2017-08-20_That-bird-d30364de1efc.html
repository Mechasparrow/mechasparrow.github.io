That bird

There were once two birds

That bird

There were once two birds

There’s a bird as black as night with beautiful feathers. I find that I stare at that bird from time to time.

Few weeks pass. That bird begins to fly upon a tree branch in the morning. That bird begins to sing to me every morning.

I’ve grown to like that bird quite a bit. I decide to sing along with her in the mornings. The mornings before the fog ever lifts.

The seasons begin to change. The leaves change from green to orange then fall. It becomes blissful spring.

That bird and I have grown closer and sing almost everyday. We fly high above the clouds away from the other birds and their songs.

A beauty of a song is hard to hear amongst the noise of others.

One day, she did not sing upon the tree branch with me.

Where has that bird gone? Has she forgotten about me? Do I exist anymore? My heart of steel begins to crack and melt.

Why am I feeling these emotions? How did I get rid of them? Why does my robotic heart ache? That bird is the cold one?

I am the mechanical sparrow. The irregular bird that was shunned and ridiculed.

Yet I was accepted. I was accepted by that bird.

Where has that bird gone?

Despair follows…

And then…

A familiar bird begins to sing on a special tree branch. Once again…

It is that bird.
