Socialism: A case for how it can and can’t work.

Hey, everybody. It’s Mechasparrow (A.K.A Michael Navazhylau) from Hammercircuit. A week back Überlegen Auge and I discussed socialism in the Hammercircuit podcast. I decided to create an article format of the episode.

Alright, lets talk about it!

Why can’t it work?

Let us start with the negatives first. The are several reasons that socialism as a government and as an ideology fall flat.

A key reason to this is that socialism requires effort and is voluntary. In a capitalist society, this does not sound fun.

Human’s are selfish organisms and like avoiding discomfort as much as possible. Work is often seen as a necessary discomfort in that sense.

There is a class of people in society that we have dubbed the “parasite class” in the podcast episode. There are people who only consume and provide no means of production whatsoever.

Another reason that it does not work is that capitalism does not benefit from socialist principles. If everyone is on equal standing, then there is no leverage to be used to achieve a profit.

Another very important point to realize is that certain nations such as America are wired against socialism from the beginning. The Constitution of the USA was written to only allow white male property owners to vote.

The nation’s voting has become more inclusive over time. This comes to show that it was originally geared to benefit the upper and wealthy class.

Why *can* it work?

Though there were many socialist pitfalls addressed in the information above. There are certain ways to make socialism work.

One way to do this is by creating a hybrid system. This would combine aspects of both socialism and capitalism.

There would still be a class system, but the class disparity would be smaller. This would be done through requiring the “parasite class” to still provide work of some kind.

If the person has the ability to work and is not hindered to do so, they will be taxed more. This is even if they can be taxed on anything.

When I speak of hindrances. I speak of anything that could prevent work in a physical or mental matter.

One example of this is pregnancy. The mother will eventually be able to begin contributing to society. This raises the aspect of implementing paid maternity leave. It is often seen that giving birth to a child is a contribution to society as well.

With altered taxes based on a labor to consumption ratio, this creates a redistribution of wealth throughout the society.

Another way for it to work is by creating a self-sustaining agricultural and scientific focused nation.

There would have to be several sacrifices of luxury. A material surplus would be needed to afford necessities such as medicine and other manufactured goods.

This would be a societal experiment in which all inhabitants of the nation take part. If an inhabitant become against the rules, they would be kindly asked to leave the nation. Since it is a societal experiment, there would be a risk that every inhabitant would have to take.

Conclusion

There are definitely ways that socialism can and can’t work. The best solution seems to find a compromise between both. Not complete capitalism. Not complete socialism. But a mixture appealing to both sides.
