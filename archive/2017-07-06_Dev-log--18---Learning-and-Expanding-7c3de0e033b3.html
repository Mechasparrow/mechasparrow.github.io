Dev log #18 — Learning and Expanding

Hey, everyone. It’s Mechasparrow a.k.a Michael Navazhylau. I’m here with another dev log after a crazy fun week. This is due to the growth of my blog and other side projects.

The three main projects this week I’ve been working on are my Back-end Certification, Technical Articles, and Desktop Programming. I also have an idea for another facet of content creation.

Let’s go into more depth about each of these.

Back-end Certification w/ FreeCodeCamp

Currently, a large chunk of my time goes towards FreeCodeCamp. Last month, I completed my certification for Data Visualization. I wrote about this in dev log #17 (https://medium.com/@mechasparrow/dev-log-17-freecodecamp-data-visualization-post-mortem-dafebdb22768).

These certifications I am working towards are to have a solid foundation in programming and web development.

This is because I will be in this High School program called Spark! Specifically, the tech portion of the program. We will be working on a variety of projects.

I would like to be as prepared as possible.

Also, it looks good on my resume :D

Technical Articles

Near the beginning of the week, I decided to begin writing more technical articles.

One of these articles caught the attention of a web development publication called codeburst.io. I was invited to become a writer for the publication, and I accepted the offer.

Through the publication, I was able to publish an article that received a high viewership. You can view the article at https://codeburst.io/how-to-desktop-application-with-electron-and-react-b606aa3cb529.

I intend on continuing to write technical articles on a weekly basis. This will make my blogging rate 3 blogs/week.

I hope to cover topics that range from beginner to more intermediate and expert concepts.

Desktop programming

The cause of one of my technical articles was due to a side project. This side project has to do with keeping track of rules and titles of games on your computer.

The usefulness of this is debatable. I’m doing it to see if I can make a desktop application with Electron. So it is more of an experiment.

You can view the project at https://github.com/Mechasparrow/game-creator

Tech News

This is something that I will begin trying out this Friday. The whole premise is to provide commentary on interesting and important news from sources such as TechCrunch, Hacker News, and Reddit. It will be hosted on my YouTube channel.

Well those were the main projects I’ve been working on this week. If you enjoyed the dev log, please be sure to like, share, and follow.

Michael Navazhylau is a Software Engineer in St.Louis who loves building cool and useful software. He is also the co-founder of Hammercircuit. You can check out his website at Mechasparrow.
