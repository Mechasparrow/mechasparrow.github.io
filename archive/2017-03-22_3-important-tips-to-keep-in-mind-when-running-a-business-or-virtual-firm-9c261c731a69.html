3 important tips to keep in mind when running a business or virtual firm

I enjoy business quite a bit. This goes from the day to day operations to the long term planning. Over the past few months, I’ve been able to gain valuable work and business experience.

Because I am interning at IT2GO2 and the website designer at the VE firm SPIRE, I’ve been able to see what goes into working at an established firm and a startup.

I hope to show some of the insights I’ve gained from the combined experience at both.

1. Always make sure everyone is on the same page.

I have witnessed work conflicts that were the result of miscommunication. Sometimes not everyone will get the memo.

It will often be in your best interest to double check that everyone is on the same. This helps reduce work conflicts and that everyone knows what he or she is working on.

A communication tool like Slack or G-suite from Google can help out with this. G-suite offers office tools that allow employees edit and work together with business documents.

This relates to my next point.

2. Try utilizing a project management tool.

This could be an online tool like Trello or Kanbanchi. It could also be a tool that is more offline and seen by everybody at the workplace.

At Spire, we use Kanbanchi due to the size of our team. Our team size is around 20 students. However, at IT2GO2 we use a combination of Trello and offline project management tools.

3. Make sure to schedule and plan ahead

At first, the virtual firm at Spire was somewhat disorganized and spontaneous since we were just starting out. This is completely similar to how IT2GO2 started out as well.

It is very crucial for a business to break out of this stage early on. A plan is needed in order to reach goals and achieve success.

A schedule is needed to fulfill the plans and goals that are set forth on a weekly or monthly basis.

A product management tool like mentioned above can sometimes be used. This can function as a company event calendar for the team as well.
