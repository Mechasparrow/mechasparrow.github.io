How to make your designs cleaner.

You’re working on your design. You furiously go through several iterations. You reach your nth design. You take a step back and think to yourself, Why does this look off? Then your designer friend tells you it’s cluttered. You look at him in bewilderment. Let’s discuss ways to make your design uncluttered.

Recall the principles of design

If you don’t remember the principles of design or are an amateur designer you can take a look here or here on wikipedia. Look at your design and see if any of elements are conflicting or overpowering each other. For example, see if your Emphasis is taking away from the Harmony or Unity of the design.

Cut out unnecessary information or elements

When the are elements or pieces of your design that seem to be placed arbitrarily, they are often called an after thought. Often these elements will negatively impact the balance and unity of a design. Taking these out of your design will help bring unity and balance back into the design as a result. These unnecessary elements can be identified by looking at the individual design elements and seeing how they look like compared to the elements surrounding it.

Don’t make the audience or viewer analyze the whole design

A traditional viewer will often start viewing from the left or bottom of a design. This makes it important to be wary of the movement of the design and its unity. An unorganized, unbalanced design will cause higher cognitive load in a person’s viewing process. As a result, they will be unable to digest the information the design presents effectively. In order to mitigate this, try to make the design predictable in order for the brain to consolidate design elements unconsciously. This will help them comprehend the information presented.
