The first St.Louis Alexa meet up.

Last Wednesday, I attended the first Alexa meet up. Alexa is the personal assistant on the Amazon Echo and Echo Dot.

We first talked about the great promise that personal assistants have and the future potential market size. Several people then preceded to talk about how they were developing and automating with Alexa.

One of the speakers worked for Amazon. They were able to give a quick crash course for developing apps for Alexa. The example project he built was able to give short facts about a specific topic.

The best part of the night, however was when I won an Echo Dot myself. That pretty much made the night. Since now I have an Echo Dot, It is my duty to make an app for it :)

Here is what I think about the Dot so far.

  1. Easy setup
  2. Nice array of features
  3. Connectivity issues

Easy setup

The Amazon Alexa app provides an intuitive interface for setup and operation of the Echo Dot on a day-to-day basis. The Dot was even told me to download the app.

Nice Array of Features

Within the first hour of using the device, I was able to setup an alarm, todo list, and play music to my Bluetooth headphones.

Connectivity issues

This is usually occurs with any device. Every once in a while the Echo Dot will lose connection to the internet.

When that occurs, it will glitch out and start acting very funky. This is probably with where I have it placed (in a corner), and the fact that my WiFi is pretty sketchy.
