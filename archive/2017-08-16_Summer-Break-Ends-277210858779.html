Summer Break Ends

For me at least.

Hello, everyone. It is Mechasparrow yet again. This is the last day of summer break for me. I will be heading back to school starting tomorrow. Because of this, I’d like to give an overview of the break and what I learned.

Overview

After the anti-climatic end to second-year high school, I was left to my own devices. It really was up to me how I would spend the summer.

I decided the first thing I would do was to hone my coding skills. I was able to pick up a new coding certificate along with some other tech skills.

The other thing I decided to do was working out. The main driving factor is so that I’m not staring at the computer all day. I built up a routine by the end of the first month of summer.

I increased the amount of writing I would perform. I eventually hit a really good niche in July. As a result, my blog has quadrupled in size.

Coding

I built some coding side projects. Nothing too large really. The bigger projects were built for my coding certificate.

I was able to get a certificate in data visualization. There were concepts that were hard to wrap around my head, but I eventually got it.

Working Out

I first started out doing a full body workout. The issue with this is that it really only focused on strength.

That meant stamina and cardio were not included. I later realized this and decided to incorporate running.

When I first started running, it was very difficult. After the first week, it became easier and more enjoyable.

Writing

I used to produce 2 articles a week back when I was in school. During summer I ramped it up to 3 articles.

I found a niche by producing coding tutorials and tech blog posts. I was invited to my first publication on Medium. One of the coding tutorials received became a big hit. It received over 100 likes.

I am also currently taking a daily blogging challenge for August. This article is the 14th in this challenge.
