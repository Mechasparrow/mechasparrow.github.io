Convincing People

What are some ways and reasons we convince people?

These are the show notes from the Hammercircuit talk show.

Here are the key points from the first episode of the Hammercircuit talk show.

Understand where they are coming from

If you are trying to change someone’s opinion, you want to know how the opinion was formed in the first place. It could have been affected by an emotional event, reading the news, or by their community.

Some people are inseparable from their opinions

There are certain people who emotionally invest so much into their opinions. This is ok when the opinion does not have much impact. What is the best sports team is a good example of this.

The debate between different views is frustrating

Everyone’s view of the world is up to our mind and perceptions. Some people may process and view the world through a different lens than you do. This causes arguments and difficulty conveying your view to others.

The key solution to this is to understand their world. This way you can build a bridge between your view and theirs.

The primal brain keeps people from being level headed in arguments

There are 2 parts of the brain in any individual. The primal brain and the logical brain. The primitive part of the brain often acts first. It takes some willpower to avoid acting on the primitive part. If you want to convince somebody of something, you want to strike their emotional chords. This is because they are affected first.

Depending on how tied to their opinions they are, it might be impossible to change their minds

Some people’s world view is so tainted and solidified that it might be impossible to change their minds.

Weigh the pros and cons when entering an argument, and know when it is time to give up. Because there are certain arguments that do not impact the world in the long run.

With difference in values, respect of those values is important

Unless the values hurt others, it is important to respect other people’s values. People decide to give up trying to understand and fight and yell. This does not get us anywhere.

The world is not black and white

The human brain is made up of around 100 billion neurons. Each segment communicates at varying degrees. This means that there is gray area with any of our thoughts and opinions. It is rarely exactly 1 or 0, left or right. It is always some sort of combination of varying proportions.
