How to survive a Virtual Enterprise trade show

The last couple of days have been crazy for me and the tech team of Spire from Virtual Enterprise. We participated in the #midwesttradeshow, and faced several successes and several obstacles. In this article, I would like to highlight some of the do’s and don’ts of Virtual Enterprise Show.

Do have fun and explore.

You’re in a new place away from home. You’re competing with firms all over the country the least you can do is reap your efforts. In Tennessee, our firm made sure to definitely reap the benefits of our trip. We visited several places during our stay. Stay close and don’t get lost. :)

Do network

This is even for the non-sales people too. You’ve been working so hard at your firm. Helping build the company like the rest of employees. Why not share the experiences with other firm employees, gain insight to how they solved problems, and meet a new colleague. You never know if the person you meet might be a gateway to a potential client or a possible partnership even if you do not specialize in sales.

Don’t (try not to) stay up late!

One thing that I would heavily recommend to firms that have a strong tech/creative focus is to be prepared ahead of time. There were things that the creative team was not able to do ahead of time. As a result of that we had to stay up long nights to attempt to solve the issues. Instead some of the issues had to be solved another way. Preparation is the best way to streamline the experience.

Be Resilient and Don’t take it Leisurely

The trade show’s only 4 hours long. However in that time we were able to make several sales and solve problems as the came. We did not halt in our wake. We did not freak out. Instead, we steamed ahead. Resilience is a key factor in business and in life. Business does not wait around. There are deadlines and time crunches. Be prepared and ready to bounce back and improvise in case of the unexpected.

With that I wish future VE participants good luck. Remember to work hard and enjoy the experience.
