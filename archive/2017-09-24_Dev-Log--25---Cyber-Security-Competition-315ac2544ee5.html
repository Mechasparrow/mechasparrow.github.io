The Competition

My colleagues and I from Spark attended the iGen Cyber Games event hosted by the Midwest Cyber Center and UMSL.

We competed against other Middle and High School teams in cybersecurity-related puzzles. By the end of the competition, our team was able to gain the most points.

The Team

The team was composed of 4 people. Each of us had comparable technical skills. Since there were different challenge categories, we had to divide and conquer.

We completed certain categories based on our individual strengths and weaknesses.

This tactic led us to gain the most points in a short period of time.

The Challenges

The challenges contained several different categories. These categories were

  • Programming
  • Cryptography
  • Steganography
  • MySQL
  • Unix

I chose to spend the bulk of my time with MySQL since it was the category I was most comfortable with.
