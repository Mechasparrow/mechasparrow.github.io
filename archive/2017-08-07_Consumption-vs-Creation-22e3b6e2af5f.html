Consumption vs Creation

Lets compare the 2 key parts of the economy.

Consumption vs Creation

Since the dawn of human civilization, there have always been consumers and creators. These two groups are locked in an ebb and flow.

Consumption can not really be done without creation. Creation can not really be done without some consumption.

Here are some comparisons.

Consumption and Creation can both be addicting

With consumption such social media and news, it is pretty addicting. They are built to grab your attention for longer and longer periods of time.

It appeals to our desire for instant gratification.

Creation is a different type of addiction. It requires a certain humility to improve. The response to a creation can be just as or even more rewarding as scrolling through a social media feed.

This is because of the longer amount of time invested towards the creation. It is not necessarily a linear relationship when it comes to time invested to reward.

There are always more consumers than there are creators

This is because it requires very little energy to check Instagram than it does to draw or write a short story. Some people may want to become a YouTuber, Writer, Artist, etc but do not want to put in the work necessary.

That is because they have only have seen the end product and not the effort involved.

Consumption is Low Risk while Creation is Medium to High Risk

Viewing a post or watching a video has almost no risk. Other people don’t even need to know that you viewed it sometimes.

Creating something new comes with several risks. This includes

  • Shame
  • Ridicule
  • No VIEWS
