The Power of the Morning Routine

Summer break has more or less started. As a result, I’ve been building up a morning routine. I’ve been getting some positive results, so I’d like to share my observations.

The Routine

The routine is as currently stands

  • Wake up
  • Meditate
  • Wash up
  • Exercise (Body and Stretching)
  • Build Agenda

Meditation has been shown to have several health benefits. I use it to clear my head and orient myself.

Exercising keeps the body healthy and fit. It also helps bash out any remnants of morning fatigue.

Benefits

I already listed some benefits above. There a couple major benefits that I have encountered. The three main ones that I found were,

  • Sense of daily achievement
  • Better workflow throughout the day
  • Initial grounding

Daily achievement

Sometimes not every day is great. With a morning routine, you can always look back and say, “I still managed to complete my morning routine”

Better workflow

As a potential result of meditation, my workflow has improved.

This could be due to the fact that most unnecessary thoughts are dealt with in the morning. This leaves my room for focused work to be done.

Initial grounding

Before I started my morning routine, I used to wake up with a foggy head.

What I mean is there was a disconnect between what I was observing and how my mind was perceiving it. It’s a hard feeling to describe.

After I started doing my morning routine with meditation, I feel more “synced” so to speak.

These are the observations that I found while building up a morning routine. If you found this article useful, please be sure to follow, share, and recommend.
