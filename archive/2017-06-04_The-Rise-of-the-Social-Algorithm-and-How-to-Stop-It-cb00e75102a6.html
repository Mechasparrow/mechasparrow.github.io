The Rise of the Social Algorithm and How to Stop It

Snapchat, Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. They’re all social media platforms, but that is not the only thing that they have in common.

They all use some form of social algorithms. You might have heard of algorithms that run on machines, but what are social algorithms?

This is what we would like to talk to you guys about.

What is the Social Algorithm?

A Social algorithm is a type of computer algorithm applied to social media platforms.

They are designed to discover what we want. As a result, they create personalized experiences.

They are also used to filter through the influx of data that is generated on those platforms. This can tend to create filter bubbles.

Filter bubbles are information provided to the user through a collection of filters. The information provided are often guesses what the human will like. This is usually without your consultation.

This leads to never seeing anything that could be against your views.

Why are Social Algorithms dangerous?

Social algorithms can be dangerous for a variety of reasons. It all stems from how they create a filter bubble that we are stuck living in when using these platforms.

This can lead to creation of a fixed mindset and lack of perspective

Fixed mindset

Fixed mindset is the belief that traits are fixed and do not change. Social algorithms cause this.

This is because they never show you content that is outside of your comfort zone. Change is often an uncomfortable experience.

Lack of perspective

Social algorithms will rarely feed you content that goes against your “digital” identity.

This makes it more difficult to find content that may change your world view.

One example of this is never seeing only liberal or conservative content.

Keep in mind that these platforms are designed to keep you there as long as possible. The endless feeds that Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter implement is one example.

How to overcome the Social algorithm?

So you might want to try broadening your horizons as a result of this. How would one start? Well you can

  • diversify interests on social media
  • ask questions on social media
  • tag users
  • Ask for engagement (ask for people to like and subscribe/follow)

The key thing is to be vigilant and not take whats given to you.
