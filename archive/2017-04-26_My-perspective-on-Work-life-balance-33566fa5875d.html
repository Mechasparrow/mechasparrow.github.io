My perspective on Work-life balance

I’ve been thinking a lot recently about work-life balance. I’m currently a high school student with an internship. Since that is the case, I though that I could shed some light on this topic from my perspective.

My answer is that they feed upon one another. Let me explain.

Work aspect

When I started working at my mother’s company, I had to do a large amount of work to setup the website. Afterward, It was mostly maintenance from there and helping write business documents.

We work for the reason that it gives us the support structure for the rest of our life that does not have to do with work. Yet, there is a secondary reason.

That reason is that work is fulfilling. We get the sense that we are making a contribution and making progress towards a goal.

Whatever that goal may be.

Whenever I am exhausted at work or burnt out from work, I often switch to alternative activities. This would include reading, writing, watching movies.

So it’s important to understand when to change gears. Change gears too late and it feels that there’s no fun being had.

My internship does not lend itself much to web development at all. Because of this, I dive into monthly side projects.

This is where work and life begin to merge.

Life aspect

Like many other teenagers my age, I have school, friends, and hobbies. The funny thing is that one of my hobbies is often confused as work.

I like building things. This would be software and electronics. Essentially, I find joy and building things that perform a task.

How can it be that I go to work and after I come home and I do more … work? That’s because what makes up the life part is the fun and good experiences.

So that’s why sometimes they can merge. Work can also be part of that collection of good experiences. It depends on what type of work you do and how you perceive it.

There it is. My view on work-life experience and how it flows. I hope that you found this article useful. If so please like, comment, and share.
