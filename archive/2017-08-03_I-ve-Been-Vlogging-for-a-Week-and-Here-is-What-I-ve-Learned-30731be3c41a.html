I’ve Been Vlogging for a Week and Here is What I’ve Learned

So I have been vlogging for about a week now. The idea to start doing daily vlogging came to me as a way to get better at video editing.

I started on the day I arrived in California and have been following a simple schedule.

It works as follows:

  • Record the vlog
  • Write a Blog post
  • Edit yesterday’s vlog
  • Publish yesterday’s vlog

Each time I make a video, I try out a new tactics.

I’d like to share with you what I have learned so far.

Sense of a good shot

As you continue publishing a vlog, you come to understand that you need to grab the attention of the viewer.

I do this by trying to record interesting shots when I get the chance. When I don’t, I try to do the best with what I got.

This goes into the next point.

Not every vlog can be the best

With how I live my life, I can’t say every moment is the most exciting.

This leads to days with large amounts of footage and days that don’t have that much.

With vlogs, it boils down to the content of the vlog itself.

Recording the vlog

I currently record the vlog on my smartphone and publish in 720p. I don’t think my camera supports 1080p, so I don’t try to release at that resolution.

I could have a dedicated camera for recording video and taking pictures. Yet, smartphones provide higher form of mobility for the quality I’m looking for.

Vlog music

It is pretty cliche, but I use some royalty free chillhop music. In later episodes I might start experimenting with the vlog music.

I usually insert the music during transitional scenes such as driving and riding trains.

I also include it when I’m not talking to the camera.

Actually editing the vlog

I have all this raw footage. How do I even make it bearable for an audience to watch?

Well my comrades it comes from the magic of editing.

It means cutting out the clips that are bad and stitching together the ones that are good. This has to be done while maintaining continuity and context as well.

That way the vlog has a story arc and does not feel random.

Since the amount of footage is pretty small it only takes about 15–30 minutes to edit.

I also learned that short films can still take quite a bit of time to edit.

Editing Setup

Now my editing setup is pretty bad right now. This is because I am currently on vacation right now.

Hardware is an HP notebook. * No I don’t know the exact one *

Software is Ubuntu 14.04 running Flowblade. Flowblade is an cross platform open source video editor.

It is not as featured as something like Adobe Premiere Pro, but it gets the job done.

Conclusion

So far, I feel like I have learned quite a bit about video editing. I will probably stop vlogging consistently after I come back from vacation.

I will take what I learned from vlogging and use it to create new type of YouTube videos.

This will aid in Hammercircuit and creating YouTube tutorials and skits.
