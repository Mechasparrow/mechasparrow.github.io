Breaking Out of Monotonous High School Life

My father once told me that in his homeland, students finished their high school education at age 16. After that, they are considered adults.

In the United States, this is not the case.

This does bring up an interesting point. After completing half of my high school education, it is already time to start looking forward.

I‘ll be doing several things to break out the monotony that is high school life. This would include Spark! Tech, Freelancing, Self-Study, High School clubs/organizations, and Engineering/Creation.

Let’s dive into each one.

Spark! Tech

At my high school, we have this program called Spark! which is defined as, “self-directed by students and are aligned to their strengths, talents, abilities, and interests.”

What this means is that I can do what I love doing and have it count towards my high school education.

By being in the Spark! Tech strand of the program, I will be able to

  • Gain field experience
  • Work with like-minded individuals and industry professions
  • Build software for the local community

Freelancing

I will also continue my work experience through freelancing. I’ll be able to use experience from my mother’s company IT2Go2 and in the Virtual Enterprise program.

Virtual Enterprise is a program that turns students into business professionals.

This will help build up my personal brand and resume all while working with a variety of people.

Self Study

Self-study covers a variety of activities. This would include philosophy (Buddhism, Taoism, etc), journaling, and material study.

Journaling helps clear the mind with the help of paper and pen.

The material study will be that of computer science, mathematics, and business. This will help grow my domain experience.

Philosophy started becoming an important part of my life near the end of my 2nd year of high school. I encountered difficult issues with my life that were hard to resolve.

As a result, I had a couple weeks of spiritual limbo. Philosophy was able to pull me out of it.

This led to many internal changes in my worldview.

Creation and Engineering

I will be continuing podcasting, devlogs, blogs, software projects.

This content creation provides value to each day in many ways. This is in contrary to what can often happen where one thinks to themselves, “Where did all that time go?”

I intend to continue the podcast, Hammercircuit with Überlegen Auge. The conversations that we have seem to have some interest.

Engineering side projects are great for building skills. If anything, engineering projects will also help when I need to do it for a salary, debt, mortgage, etc.

High School clubs/organizations

This is something that High Schools actually tend to recommend. This is nice of them.

I will start a Hack Club at my high school. Along with this, I will be tutoring through Mu Alpha Theta, the math honors society. I will also take part in hackathons and competitions with my fellow students. This will present unique challenges with valuable life lessons.
