The Spider-Man: Homecoming Review and Discussion

Hey. It’s your friendly neighborhood Mechasparrow. I know that Spider-Man: Homecoming has already been out for almost a month now, but I’d still like to do a review.

Here it goes.

Context

Spider-Man: Homecoming starts a little after the events of Avengers: Civil War. Peter Parker (a.k.a Spider-Man) is excited for the next mission that never came.

Throughout the film, he constantly tries to prove to his mentor, Tony Stark. This leads to several conflicts arising throughout the film.

Character analysis

Many of the characters within the film are fleshed out. This is especially true with our main character, Peter Parker, and his arch-enemy in the film, the Vulture.

Each of them is given their own worries, desires, personality, and quirks.

Character Development

The main character, Peter Parker, is constantly looking for adventure. Tony Stark warns him that he is too young and inexperienced. Peter Parker decided to not heed his warning and decided to try anyway.

This leads to many conflicts down the road. It also leads to his realization near the end of the movie that he may have bitten off more than he could chew.

He makes the decision to stay as the “Friendly Neighborhood Spider-man” at the end of the film.

Secondary-characters such as Peter Parker’s best friend are used to show that even regular civilians and humans can be heroes.

Comedy and Humor

Compared to the last 2 versions of the cinematic Spider-Man, this one was filled with an equal amount of humor and seriousness.

We see strength against overwhelming odds, yet we also see the drama and fun that is high school life.

This breathes fresh air and relevancy into the newest version of Spider-Man.

Conclusion

If you are a big fan of Marvel or Spider-Man, I would highly recommend seeing this film if you have not already. I would personally rate this film 8.5/10
