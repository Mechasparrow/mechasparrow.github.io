Ways that computer/programming clubs can help increase participation in STEM

The amount of STEM related careers are increasing in the United States and other countries. With the amount of STEM jobs needed in future, how can we insure there will be enough people in STEM? One possible way is pushing for more STEM related clubs in High Schools.

Why should there people programming and computer clubs in high schools?

The way high school students often explore their career paths is by taking classes that interest them. What is taught in the class does always interest the student and could possibly discourage potential students. Clubs on the other hand don’t have as strict of a structure as a class does. This works great for the way STEM related careers work. With STEM in order to get better, sometimes your prototype needs to fail. Here are some potential opportunities that a STEM club could provide.

1. Civic projects (non profits/community projects)

Students and club members. Can work on engineering problems for non profits and community projects. This could include software, and architectural problems. Sometimes outside input into a community project is a real help. One example of an event that helped to solve a civic problem was GlobalHack VI.

2. Side projects

Students are provided with the resources to for them to work on problems that they find interesting. Another side benefit the ability to work with peers. New ideas and solutions can form by brainstorming in a group.

3. Competitions

Students can participate in engineering and software competitions such as GlobalHack and FIRST Robotics. This can help students encounter industry problems.

These opportunities provide students with the perspective of how STEM is interesting and how it is utilized in the real world.
