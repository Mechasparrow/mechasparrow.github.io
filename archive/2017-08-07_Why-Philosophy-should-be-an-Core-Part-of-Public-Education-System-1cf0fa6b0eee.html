Why Philosophy should be an Core Part of Public Education System

My friend (Überlegen Auge) and I have been studying philosophy recently.

A thought came to my mind.

Why don’t we teach philosophy as a required course?

There are several reasons that that philosophy should be taught as a core class.

Here they are.

Philosophy teaches us how to think and reason

One of the key concepts that philosophy teaches us is how to use logic and reason. How often do we have to prove or give the reason for something?

It is very often. This makes it useful for any argument, discussion, or problem you may encounter.

Philosophy forces us to confront difficult questions

We are told to study so we can get to college, so we can get a job and etc. Something of that sort at least. When encountering questions such as

  • What is the meaning of life?
  • How much freedom should people have?
  • Should we fear death?

This breaks us out of the monotonous partial loop of school. It requires us to think outside the scope of our everyday life.

Prepares us face future difficult problems

No matter who you are, you will encounter several obstacles throughout your life. Philosophy can provide a good starting point when dealing with these problems.

This is because sometimes certain problems need a certain frame of mind to solve.
