Devlog #8 — Story of Emojilog and what’s next

Hey, everyone. Made it past the EOCs in my high school. While I had some down time, I managed to build an app called Emojilog. I’d like to explain the process that went into building the initial app from start to finish. Not only that, but I would like to talk about how I launched it. I also have some future projects to talk about as well.

Conception of Emojilog

I was watching a video while I had the idea for Emojilog. I was thinking about the best way to express my daily experiences in writing and I realized it could be summed up with emoji. I once read an article about how emoji has been getting closer and closer to becoming its own language. I thought to myself, “why not give it a shot?” That’s when Emojilog was born.

Designing the app

After deciding to build the app, I started by designing a mockup. That way I knew what my end goal would be. I decided to use Figma, an online design alternative to Sketch as my main design tool. I also chose my color scheme with the new material design tool from Google. I tried using the material design principles provided by Google. After that, I started building.

Development

I tried building my app on glitch.com. But, that led to unnecessary complexity. I decided to switch off it and use the vue-cli. Emojilog was built with the Vue.js library using the vue-cli. It also uses emoji-css from afeld for its emoji usage. The storage solution is the localstorage object provided in most web browsers.

After tying these libraries together I was able to build the first version of emojilog.

Deployment

For deployment, I used surge.sh, a front-end deployment platform. All I had to do was type, “surge” in my distribution folder of my code to deploy the app.

Launch

After the app went live, I posted to the following locations.

  • /r/SideProject
  • /r/shamelessplug
  • /r/IMadeThis
  • Hacker News
  • Designer News

I posted to /r/SideProject late at night yesterday. Overnight I received 7 stars on my GitHub repository. That’s when I realized I forgot to install analytics. A little after Sunday morning started, I installed Google Analytics. I was able to watch the traffic that was coming into the website.

I also realized I spelled emoji wrong.

What’s next for Emojilog?

Considering how much feedback I got with the app’s initial release, I will continue to develop it. I would like to create a medical version of the app that could better track patients with mental disorders. This would include mental disorders such as Bipolar disorder, PTSD, and Depression.

Other projects?

Yeah, tons. I have several ideas that are on my trello board right now. Currently, I’m thinking about creating an app for curated workflows. It would be a simple list of links that would all open at the same time. That or another simple generator/library for project names.

I hoped that you enjoyed the latest devlog on Emojilog. If you found the article useful, please make sure to like, follow, and share.
