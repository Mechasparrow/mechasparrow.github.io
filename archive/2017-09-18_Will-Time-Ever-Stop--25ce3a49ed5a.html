Will Time Ever Stop?

Mechasparrow asks a really vague question.

The answer is maybe.

You see… Time is relative.

Because Einstein

Time does not really mean anything without entropy

Entropy is defined as the

a thermodynamic quantity representing the unavailability of a system’s thermal energy for conversion into mechanical work, often interpreted as the degree of disorder or randomness in the system.

or how

an ordered system declines into disorder.

So if everything eventually become uniformly in disorder, we get a heat death.

Time will not have anything to act upon because everything is at a state of perfect balance.

This means that life will no longer function because it requires ordered systems to interact with an environment of disorder (Earth) or natural resources.

Same with computers. Data is stored as a organized sequence of states. When they fall into disorder, it means nothing.

So in hindsight, the world will be at perfect balance within several eons and then maybe… just maybe. Time will stop.

But… We will all be gone by that time.
